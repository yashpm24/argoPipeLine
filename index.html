The Milky Way Galaxy

Our Home galaxy is a vast spiral of stars, dust, and dark matter. It contains over 100 billion stars, with our Solar System tucked away in one of its spiral arms. On a clear night, the Milky Way can be seen as a glowing band stretching across the sky.

The Sun

The Sun is the heart of our Solar System, a massive ball of glowing plasma. It provides the heat and light necessary for life on Earth. Its energy comes from nuclear fusion at its core, where hydrogen atoms fuse into helium, releasing immense amounts of energy.

Stars

Stars are the building blocks of galaxies. They come in many sizes, colors, and ages. From tiny red dwarfs to massive blue giants, stars shape the universe with their light and energy. Some end their lives as spectacular supernovae, creating the elements that make up planets and even life.

The Moon

Earth's only natural satellite, the Moon influences our tides and has been a source of wonder for millennia. Its surface is covered with craters and plains, remnants of ancient impacts. In 1969, humans first set foot on its surface, marking a new chapter in space exploration.
